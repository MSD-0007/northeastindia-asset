UMIAM LAKE

About The Location

Umiam Lake, also known as "Bara Pani" (meaning Big Water), is a large, man-made reservoir located in the state of Meghalaya, about 15 kilometers north of Shillong. It is one of the most popular and picturesque tourist destinations in northeastern India. Surrounded by rolling hills, dense pine forests, and lush greenery, Umiam Lake offers a tranquil retreat for nature lovers, adventure seekers, and anyone looking to escape the hustle and bustle of city life.

Location and Formation

Umiam Lake was created in the 1960s as part of a damming project on the Umiam River. The lake was formed as a result of the construction of the Umiam Hydroelectric Dam, which was built to generate hydroelectric power for the region. The dam, one of the first of its kind in northeastern India, led to the submergence of a large area, creating this vast reservoir that spans over 220 square kilometers.

The lake is situated at an altitude of around 1,000 meters above sea level, adding to its cool and refreshing atmosphere. It lies within the Umiam Watershed, which plays an important role in regulating the water supply for both hydroelectric power generation and irrigation.

Scenic Beauty

Umiam Lake is renowned for its breathtaking scenic beauty. Surrounded by the East Khasi Hills and forested landscapes, the lake's shimmering waters reflect the blue sky and verdant surroundings, creating postcard-perfect views. The changing light throughout the day – from the golden hues of sunrise to the soft glow of sunset – makes the lake a photographer's paradise. Mist often settles over the lake, giving it an ethereal, dreamlike quality.

The lake is dotted with small islands and bordered by pine and coniferous forests. The serene environment and cool weather make Umiam Lake an ideal spot for picnics, relaxation, and unwinding amidst nature.

Adventure and Water Sports

Umiam Lake is also a hub for various water-based adventure activities, which attract tourists and adventure enthusiasts year-round. Some of the popular activities include:

  • Boating: Pedal boats, speed boats, and rowboats are available for visitors to explore the lake’s calm waters.
  • Kayaking and Canoeing: For those seeking a more immersive experience, kayaking and canoeing offer a unique way to explore the lake's tranquil waters.
  • Water Skiing: This adrenaline-pumping sport is available for thrill-seekers looking to glide across the water's surface.
  • Jet Skiing: Visitors can enjoy fast-paced rides across the lake on jet skis, offering a fun and adventurous experience.
  • Sailing and Yachting: Umiam Lake is also a destination for sailing enthusiasts, with opportunities for sailing and yachting during suitable weather conditions.

In addition to water sports, there are adventure activities like camping, trekking, and rock climbing around the lake. The region's undulating hills and lush forests provide ample opportunities for outdoor exploration.

Nehru Park

Located along the banks of Umiam Lake, Nehru Park is a beautifully maintained garden that adds to the charm of the area. It offers well-manicured lawns, flower gardens, and viewpoints from which visitors can enjoy panoramic views of the lake and its surroundings. The park is ideal for leisurely walks, picnics, and relaxation. There are also facilities for recreational activities like angling and boating.

Flora and Fauna

The ecosystem around Umiam Lake is rich in biodiversity, supporting a wide variety of plant and animal species. The surrounding pine and coniferous forests are home to numerous birds, making it a haven for birdwatchers. Common sightings include kingfishers, herons, and other waterfowl. The lake also serves as a habitat for fish species, which contribute to the local fishing industry.

Environmental Concerns

As a man-made reservoir, Umiam Lake plays a crucial role in the region’s water management, but it also faces environmental challenges. Pollution from human activity, agricultural runoff, and occasional littering has raised concerns about the lake’s long-term ecological health. Conservation efforts are needed to maintain the pristine beauty and water quality of the lake, ensuring that it remains a sustainable destination for both locals and tourists.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Umiam Lake is during the months of October to May, when the weather is pleasant and the skies are clear. During these months, the lake offers stunning views, and the calm conditions are perfect for water sports and outdoor activities. The monsoon season (June to September) sees heavy rainfall, which may affect accessibility, but it also turns the surrounding landscape into a lush green haven.

Accessibility and Facilities

Umiam Lake is easily accessible by road, located about 15 kilometers from Shillong on the Shillong-Guwahati Highway (NH6). Visitors traveling from Shillong can reach the lake in about 30 minutes by car or taxi. The lake is well-connected and a popular stopover for tourists heading to Shillong from Guwahati.

The Meghalaya Tourism Department has developed several facilities around the lake to accommodate visitors. There are boating clubs, water sports equipment rental services, rest houses, and cottages for those who wish to stay overnight. The Umiam Lake View Resort and Orchid Lake Resort are two popular accommodations offering lakefront views and modern amenities.

Nearby Attractions

  • Shillong: The capital city of Meghalaya, located just 15 kilometers away, offers numerous attractions, including Ward's Lake, Shillong Peak, Don Bosco Museum, and vibrant local markets.
  • Mawphlang Sacred Grove: A preserved ancient forest rich in biodiversity and cultural significance, located around 25 kilometers from Umiam Lake.
  • Elephant Falls: A multi-tiered waterfall located about 20 kilometers from the lake, known for its scenic beauty and serene atmosphere.
  • Shillong Golf Course: One of the oldest and most scenic golf courses in India, located near Umiam Lake.

Conclusion

Umiam Lake is much more than just a scenic reservoir – it is a vital water source, an adventure hub, and a serene retreat for visitors. Its combination of natural beauty, recreational activities, and accessibility make it one of Meghalaya’s premier tourist destinations. Whether you are seeking relaxation, adventure, or a place to connect with nature, Umiam Lake offers a perfect escape into the heart of Meghalaya’s picturesque landscape.
