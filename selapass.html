SELAPASS

About The Location

Sela Pass, located at an altitude of 13,700 feet (4,170 meters) in Arunachal Pradesh, India, is a breathtaking mountain pass that connects the towns of Tawang and Dirang. It is known for its stunning views, spiritual significance, and adventure opportunities, making it a popular destination for tourists and nature enthusiasts.

Mesmerizing Landscape

The landscape around Sela Pass is dominated by snow-capped mountains, lush valleys, and crystal-clear lakes. During winter, the entire area transforms into a snowy wonderland, while summer offers cool alpine air and vibrant greenery, perfect for visitors looking to escape the heat.

Sela Lake

At the heart of the pass lies Sela Lake, also known as Paradise Lake or Tso Lhamo Lake. This glacial lake reflects the snow-covered peaks and offers a serene, picturesque setting. The lake freezes in winter, creating a magical scene, while in summer, its deep blue waters provide a peaceful spot for contemplation and photography.

Spiritual Significance

The area around Sela Pass holds deep spiritual significance for the local Buddhist community. It is believed that the pass is named after Sela, a tribal woman who, alongside Indian soldier Jaswant Singh Rawat, played a heroic role during the 1962 Sino-Indian War. The nearby Jaswant Garh War Memorial honors these brave figures and is a site of reverence for visitors.

Adventure and Trekking

Adventure lovers flock to Sela Pass for its thrilling high-altitude treks. The rugged terrain, combined with unpredictable weather, makes for an exciting challenge. However, due to the high altitude, tourists are advised to be aware of acute mountain sickness (AMS) and take necessary precautions.

Flora and Fauna

The region boasts rich biodiversity, with the surrounding forests home to unique species of rhododendrons, alpine flowers, and rare medicinal plants. Wildlife enthusiasts may also spot elusive animals like the red panda, Himalayan monal, and snow leopard.

Best Time to Visit

Though accessible throughout the year, the ideal time to visit Sela Pass is between March and October. Summer and early autumn offer pleasant weather and clear skies for the best views. Winter brings heavy snowfall, turning the landscape into a frozen paradise, but travelers should be prepared for road closures due to snow.

How to Reach

Sela Pass is located approximately 78 kilometers from Tawang and 340 kilometers from Tezpur. The nearest airport is at Salonibari Airport (Tezpur), and the nearest railway station is also in Tezpur. Tourists can hire private vehicles or taxis to reach Sela Pass, enjoying scenic views of dense forests and valleys along the way.

Travel Tips

  • Permits: Domestic and international tourists require special Inner Line Permits (ILP) and Protected Area Permits (PAP) to visit Sela Pass and other areas in Arunachal Pradesh.
  • Altitude: Due to the high altitude, visitors should be cautious of altitude sickness. Carry appropriate medications, stay hydrated, and allow time for acclimatization.
  • Weather: Weather can change rapidly, especially in winter, so it's essential to carry warm clothing even during the summer months.

Nearby Attractions

  • Tawang Monastery: The largest Buddhist monastery in India, located about 80 kilometers from Sela Pass, is a must-visit for spiritual seekers.
  • Jaswant Garh War Memorial: Pay tribute to the brave soldiers of the 1962 Sino-Indian War at this historical site near the pass.
  • Nuranang Waterfall: Also known as Jang Waterfall, this stunning cascade near Tawang offers an ideal spot for photography and relaxation.
